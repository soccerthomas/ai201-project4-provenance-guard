# AI Provenance Guard - Planning

## Detection Signals

### Signal 1: Groq LLM Assessment

**What it measures**

* Uses the Groq `llama-3.3-70b-versatile` model to estimate how likely a piece of text is AI-generated based on writing style and language patterns.

**Output**

* Decimal score between **0.0 and 1.0**.

  * 0.0 = very likely human
  * 1.0 = very likely AI

**Blind spots**

* Edited AI text may appear human.
* Highly polished human writing may resemble AI.
* Performance depends on the LLM's training.

---

### Signal 2: Stylometric Heuristics

**What it measures**

* Average sentence length
* Vocabulary diversity
* Word repetition
* Burstiness (variation in sentence lengths)
* Punctuation frequency

**Output**

* Decimal score between **0.0 and 1.0** indicating similarity to common AI writing characteristics.

**Blind spots**

* Poems or technical writing often use repetitive structures.
* Very short text provides little stylistic information.
* Creative prompting can make AI writing appear more human.

---

### Combining Signals

The final confidence score is the weighted average:

```text
Confidence = (0.6 × Groq Score) + (0.4 × Heuristic Score)
```

The Groq assessment receives slightly more weight because it captures broader contextual patterns than simple heuristics.

# Uncertainty Representation

The confidence score represents the estimated probability that the submitted text was AI-generated.

Score Ranges:

| Confidence  | Interpretation |
| ----------- | -------------- |
| 0.00 – 0.39 | Likely Human   |
| 0.40 – 0.69 | Uncertain      |
| 0.70 – 1.00 | Likely AI      |

A score of **0.60** indicates mixed evidence. Neither signal is strong enough to confidently classify the text, so the system presents an "Uncertain" result rather than making a definitive claim.

# Transparency Label Design

### High Confidence AI

**Likely AI Generated**

Confidence: XX%

Our analysis found strong indicators commonly associated with AI-generated writing. This result is probabilistic and should not be treated as definitive proof.

---

### High Confidence Human

**Likely Human Written**

Confidence: XX%

Our analysis found stronger evidence of human writing patterns than AI-generated patterns. This result is probabilistic and not a guarantee.

---

### Uncertain

**Uncertain**

Confidence: XX%

The available evidence is mixed. The system cannot confidently determine whether this content was written by a human or generated by AI.

# Appeals Workflow

Any creator whose submission has been analyzed may submit an appeal.

Required information:

* content_id
* creator_id
* appeal reason

When an appeal is submitted:

1. The appeal is stored in the audit log.
2. Submission status changes from **Classified** to **Pending Review**.
3. Timestamp and appeal reason are recorded.
4. A confirmation response is returned.

A human reviewer will see:

* Original text
* Creator ID
* Groq score
* Heuristic score
* Final confidence
* Assigned label
* Appeal reason
* Submission timestamp

---

# Anticipated Edge Cases

### Edge Case 1

A poem with heavy repetition and simple vocabulary could resemble AI writing even though it was written by a person.

---

### Edge Case 2

A human-edited AI draft may contain enough natural variation that it receives a "Likely Human" classification.

---

### Edge Case 3

Very short submissions (one or two sentences) provide too little information for reliable stylometric analysis.

# Architecture

## Submission Flow

```text
User
  |
  | Raw Text
  v
POST /submit
  |
  v
Groq Detection
  |
  | Groq Score
  v
Stylometric Analysis
  |
  | Heuristic Score
  v
Confidence Scoring
  |
  | Combined Score
  v
Transparency Label
  |
  +------> SQLite Audit Log
  |
  v
JSON Response
  |
  v
User
```

## Appeal Flow

```text
User
  |
  | content_id + reason
  v
POST /appeal
  |
  v
Status Update
  |
  +------> SQLite Audit Log
  |
  v
Confirmation Response
```

### Architecture Narrative

When a user submits text, the Flask backend receives it through the `/submit` endpoint. The text is analyzed by the Groq model and the stylometric heuristic module. Their scores are combined into a confidence score, converted into a transparency label, logged in SQLite, and returned to the user.

If the creator disagrees with the result, they can submit an appeal through `/appeal`. The submission status changes to **Pending Review**, the appeal is logged, and the reviewer can examine the original text and all detection results.
